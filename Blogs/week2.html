Reflection on the reading

Moulthrop's exploration of hypertext in "You Say You Want a Revolution? Hypertext and the Laws of Media (1991)" offers a nuanced perspective on its impact, resonating deeply with the modern internet. His analysis, rooted in McLuhan's framework, highlights hypertext's disruptive potential and its parallels with today's digital environment. The internet, as a manifestation of hypertext, exemplifies its non-linear structure, enabling decentralized communication and information sharing. Moulthrop's insights prompt reflection on contemporary phenomena like social media and online communities, showcasing how hypertext continues to shape discourse and knowledge dissemination. However, the essay also prompts consideration of modern challenges such as information overload and algorithmic biases, underscoring the ongoing relevance of Moulthrop's ideas in navigating the complexities of the digital age.

Reflecting on the Interaction and the www

The video was quite easy to digest and understand, I for a long time had always looked at the design of things. This is also where I learned about the word “Ergonomic” from my EGD class in high school. I think this relates in the same way with the whole situation of the door. What I think we are meant to take from the video is our design and its effectiveness to the user.

The design should be easy to understand, intuitive in a sense. To me interaction refers to the reciprocal action between two things, whether it be two entities or a man and a machine. So to relate it to our process, there must be interaction between the user and the website. Whether it is through the changing of a page when a link clicked, or even to show the link in another colour to show that the link or page has already been opened.
